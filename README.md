## Data Used

**Data** - Sales Data with over 4116 rows from the year 2017 to 2020.

**Data Cleaning & Analysis** - Power Query

**Data Visualization** - PowerBI


## Requirements

- Core Profitability: What are the total sales, total cost, and total profit figures for the entire business during the specified period?
- Profit Margins: How does our total cost percentage compare to our total sales percentage in terms of overall profitability?
- Customer Value: Who are our top customers, and what is the breakdown of the sales, cost, and profit associated with each individual client?
- Product Performance: Which product sub-categories (e.g., Bookcases, Chairs, Appliances) are the primary drivers of sales volume?
- Inventory Focus: Which sub-categories, such as Labels or Fasteners, are contributing the least to our total sales revenue?
- Market Presence: In which European countries do we currently have an active business presence, and what is the scale of operations in each?
- Regional Volume: How is the total quantity sold distributed across our primary regions (Central, South, and North)?
- Dominant Regions: Which region accounts for more than half of our total quantity sold?
- Order Growth: How has the count of total orders fluctuated year-over-year and quarter-over-quarter from 2017 through 2020?
- Peak Periods: Are there specific years or quarters where we see a significant spike or drop in order volume?
- Logistics Preferences: What is the preferred "Ship Mode" for our customers (Economy, Economy Plus, or Immediate)?
- Time Sensitivity: Can stakeholders filter the entire dashboard to view performance for a specific date range, such as a single fiscal year?
- Service Level Analysis: How do our financial and regional KPIs change when we isolate specific shipping modes like "Immediate" delivery?

**2. Customer & Product Insights**

- Customer Value: The "List of Company Customers" table identifies top clients such as Aaron Davey (Profit: 743) and Abby Mei (Profit: 440), providing a granular breakdown of sales, cost, and profit for each.
- Product Performance: Bookcases, Chairs, and Appliances are the primary drivers of sales volume, as evidenced by the "KPIs by Sub Categories" bar chart.
- Inventory Focus: Sub-categories such as Labels, Fasteners, Paper, and Envelopes contribute the least to total sales revenue.

**3. Geographic & Operational Distribution**

- Market Presence: The "Business by Countries Served" map indicates an active presence across Europe, including major markets in the United Kingdom, France, Italy, Spain, and Germany.
- Regional Volume: The "Quantity Sold by Region" pie chart shows that sales are divided among three primary regions: Central, South, and North.
- Dominant Regions: The Central region is clearly dominant, accounting for 55.33% (5K units) of the total quantity sold.

**4. Temporal Trends & Shipping**

- Order Growth: The "Total Orders by every Year & Quarter" line chart shows a general upward trend from 2017 to 2020, though it experienced several significant fluctuations.
- Peak Periods: A major spike in order volume occurred in the fourth quarter of 2020, reaching the highest point in the four-year period (near 250 orders).
- Logistics Preferences: The "Ship Mode" slicer lists Economy, Economy Plus, and Immediate as the available options for customer delivery.

**5. Interactive Filtering Requirements**

- Time Sensitivity: The dashboard includes an Order Date slider (set from 1/1/2017 to 12/31/2020) that allows stakeholders to isolate performance for any specific date range or fiscal year.
- Service Level Analysis: By using the Ship Mode slicer, users can dynamically update all financial and regional visuals to see how KPIs differ specifically for "Immediate" or "Economy" service levels.
